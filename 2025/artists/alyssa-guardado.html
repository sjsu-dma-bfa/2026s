Fading Embrace

“Fading Embrace” explores the bittersweet nature of clinging to something beautiful, even as it drains us. The skeletal hand grips a once-vibrant rose, slowly absorbing its beauty, leaving the petals pale and lifeless. This transformation symbolizes the pain of holding onto something that was never meant to last—something we cherish but that ultimately harms us. Inspired by Proverbs 3:5-6, “Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to Him, and He will make your paths straight,” this piece serves as a reminder that true peace comes from surrender. Holding on may feel safe, but sometimes, what we refuse to release is what keeps us from growing. The more we grip, the more we lose ourselves, until we are left as empty as the thing we tried to preserve. Through this work, I reflect on the necessity of letting go—not as a loss, but as a step toward transformation. Trusting God means believing that even after release, we will be restored. It is in surrender that we find renewal, and in faith that we discover beauty beyond what we once thought we needed.
