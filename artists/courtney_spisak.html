About the Artist

Courtney Spisak is a Digital Media artist who produces 3D models made in Blender. Her choice of technique is to create high-poly 3D models using digital sculpting and then converting them to low-poly. She has begun incorporating a series of media in her work, bridging a gap between the digital and the physical. She starts with the digital file, 3D prints it, and converts it into a physical object. Her work centers around philosophical topics, primarily those that concern existential or ethical themes.

The Myth of Margery

I am an artist who was born and raised in the San Fransico Bay Area. My artwork is primarily digital 3D modeling, but can also encompass physical sculptures and bead weaving. The primary tool I use for art is Blender. I am very experimental in my art practice, and I enjoy subverting the viewer's expectations. Growing up, I was always fascinated by horror movies and their use of metaphor to explore elements of life. I pull from elements of horror and philosophy to expose the rawness of the human condition by personifying these concepts through strange creatures. I often aim to explore what it means to be a part of this world in my work. I hope to make a positive change through art. I liken the process of art creation to the process of life. Every choice in life is intentional. I embrace every mistake, as it is part of the process of life and growth.
