About the Artist

Emily Stegmeir was born and raised in San Jose, California. There is a theme of poking at reality/everyday life though a fantastical world in her works. The works are currently using blender, and after-effects as the main programs in use. She is interested in continuing and building on the unreality of her works. started with an art degree from San Jose City College. Currently attending San Jose State University in the Digital Media Art program and graduating in Spring 2026.

Glass House

In my work, Glass House, the work is currently using blender, and JavaScript code as the main programs in use. I am interested in continuing and building on the unreality of my works. The 3D figures are made in blender. The open world of the project that holds everything and lets the viewer move is A-Frame. The A-Frame lets everything be laid out for the viewer to look around. The viewer can freely walk from the starting point and see the work in 3D
